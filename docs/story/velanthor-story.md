# PART I: WORLD LORE

## The History of Velanthor

### The Age of Ash (2,000 years ago)

Before the kingdoms of men rose, before the elves retreated to their silver forests, before the dwarves descended into their mountain halls—there was only the **Void Kingdom**.

The Void Kingdom was not a realm of darkness, but of *absence*. Its people learned to bind the empty spaces between stars to their will. They carved cities from solidified nothing, summoned armies from the spaces between heartbeats. Their king, **Aethon the Undying**, wore a crown forged from the first moment of silence the world had ever known.

The Void Kingdom lasted three thousand years.

Then came the **Sundering**.

No one knows what triggered it—some say Aethon's own magic grew too powerful, others whisper of an external force, a god from beyond the void itself. What is known is this: the Void Kingdom collapsed in a single instant. Aethon died—or was unmade—and his crown shattered into seven pieces that scattered across the world.

The survivors fled into the material plane, establishing the first human settlements. The elves emerged from their forests to witness the collapse. The dwarves emerged from their mountains to find their god-king dead. The world as we know it began in the ashes of that ancient ruin.

### The First Kingdoms (1,800 - 800 years ago)

For a thousand years, humanity struggled to survive. The survivors of the Sundering were scattered, traumatized, and powerless. The elves, who had watched from their forests, offered guidance but not aid. "You chose to bind the void," their elders said. "Now live with the consequences."

Gradually, humanity rebuilt. The **Kingdom of Valdris** rose in the fertile river valleys of the east. The **Iron Clans** united under mountain-dwelling warriors in the north. The **Merchant Republic of Theris** built wealth through trade along the southern coast. The **Ashen Wastes** in the west remained uninhabited—a scarred reminder of the old world.

Eight hundred years ago, a warlord named **Marcus Valdris** conquered all three kingdoms. He united them under one banner, claiming descent from the old void mages. He called himself **Emperor**, and for two hundred years, the **Velanthor Empire** ruled supreme.

Marcus wore a crown—not of void, but of iron and gold. He had no interest in the old powers. He built an empire of law, order, and iron discipline. The Empire lasted two hundred years.

Then came the **Night of Black Flames**.

In a single night, every member of the Valdris bloodline died. Emperors, princes, princesses, infants—all dead. The Empire fractured into the kingdoms we know today: **Valdoria**, **Northreach**, **Theris**, **Ironhall**, and the **Ashen Marches**. No one knows who killed the Valdris line, or why.

But whispers persist in dark places.

The Valdris family had been searching for the seven pieces of Aethon's crown.

### The Present Day (Year 847 of the New Reckoning)

The Five Kingdoms claw at each other's throats. Border skirmishes are constant. Trade wars devastate the Theris merchants. Ironhall's king grows old and his three sons fight for succession. Valdoria, the largest kingdom, eyes its neighbors with hunger.

And in the shadows, something stirs.

Seven pieces of the Void Crown have been found in the past decade. The religious order of the **Silent Church** claims the crown is a holy relic—the crown of the first martyr, Aethon, who gave himself to seal away a greater evil. The Church preaches that reuniting the crown will bring about a new age of peace.

But there are those who believe differently.

The **Cult of the Hollow** believes Aethon was not a martyr but a prisoner—a god-king whose seal keeps something terrible at bay. They believe the crown, when united, will free their master.

The **Circle of Mages** has remained neutral, but their ancient texts mention the crown. They know what it truly is: a weapon capable of unmaking reality itself.

And the **Ashen Wastes** grow larger each year. Something leaks from the old void kingdom. The borders of the waste advance by miles each decade. The kingdoms pretend not to notice.

## The Five Kingdoms

### Valdoria

The largest and most powerful kingdom. Ruled by **Queen Isadora III**, a cunning woman who inherited a fractured court. Valdoria controls the central river valleys and the major trade roads. Its knights are legendary, its bureaucracy legendary inefficient. The capital, **Crownhaven**, houses the old imperial palace—still standing, still empty, waiting for a true emperor.

**Resources**: Agriculture, knights, bureaucracy
**Military**: 50,000 soldiers
**Religion**: The Silent Church (state religion)

### Northreach

A harsh northern land of forests and mountains. The people are tough, superstitious, and fiercely independent. Ruled by **Jarl Thorvold**, an aging warrior who Unified the clans through blood and fire. Northreach has no interest in southern politics—until threatened.

**Resources**: Furs, lumber, mining, warriors
**Military**: 20,000 berserkers and hunters
**Religion**: Old Ways (spirit worship, druidic)

### Theris

A merchant republic on the southern coast. No king or queen—instead, the **Council of Seven** governs through trade agreements and gold. Theris controls the sea trade and the banking systems. Their navy is unmatched. They sell weapons to all sides of every conflict.

**Resources**: Trade, banking, navy, manufacturing
**Military**: 10,000 professional soldiers, 200 warships
**Religion**: Coin Faith (god is wealth)

### Ironhall

Mountain-dwelling warriors who value honor above all. The current king, **Gorath Ironfist**, has three sons: **Aldric** (military commander), **Brom** (religious leader), and **Duncan** (diplomat). The succession crisis will come soon. Ironhall's dwarven-forged weapons are the best in the world.

**Resources**: Smithing, mining, fortress-cities
**Military**: 30,000 elite infantry
**Religion**: The Code of Steel

### The Ashen Marches

A buffer zone between civilization and the Ashen Wastes. Lawless, dangerous, and full of refugees. The Marcher Lords rule their small territories with absolute power. The **Watch**—a group of mercenary knights—protects the border from the things that emerge from the waste.

**Resources**: Danger, opportunity, slaves
**Military**: 5,000 (but each Marcher has private armies)
**Religion**: Survival of the fittest

## Major Factions

### The Silent Church

The dominant religion of Velanthor. They preach that **Aethon the Undying** was a martyr who sacrificed himself to seal away the **Void Beast**—an entity of pure annihilation. The Church believes reuniting the seven crown pieces will bring about an age of peace.

- **Leader**: High Seer Morath
- **Goal**: Collect all crown pieces, unite them in the Grand Cathedral
- **Methods**: Conversion, political pressure, occasional assassination
- **Tone**: Benevolent but authoritarian

### The Cult of the Hollow

A secretive organization that believes the opposite of the Silent Church. They believe Aethon was a tyrant whose imprisonment of the Void Beast was actually an act of theft—the Void Beast was the true god, and Aethon stole its divinity.

- **Leader**: Unknown (the "Hollow King")
- **Goal**: Reunite the crown, free the Void Beast
- **Methods**: Murder, dark rituals, corruption
- **Tone**: Evil, fanatical

### The Circle of Mages

An ancient order of arcane practitioners. They do not worship gods—they study the fundamental forces of reality. The Circle knows the truth about the crown: it is neither holy nor evil. It is a tool. And tools can be used by whoever holds them.

- **Leader**: The Archmage (title, not name—rotates every 20 years)
- **Goal**: Prevent anyone from using the crown, study its power
- **Methods**: Secrecy, education, intervention
- **Tone**: Neutral, academic

### The Shadow Guild

A thieves' guild that operates across all five kingdoms. They steal information, not gold. Their motto: "Knowledge is the only currency that cannot be devalued." The Guild has no interest in the crown directly—but they sell information to anyone who pays.

- **Leader**: The Eye (always anonymous)
- **Goal**: Profit, information control
- **Methods**: Espionage, theft, blackmail
- **Tone**: Neutral, mercenary

## Magic System

Magic in Velanthor comes from three sources:

### Void Magic (Rare, Dangerous)

The magic of the old world. Those who bind void energy risk losing themselves—becoming hollow, empty, with no identity beyond the magic they wield. Void mages can bend reality, erase existence, and walk between shadows. But each use slowly consumes them.

**Users**: Kira Nightwind (main character), certain Cult members

### Elemental Magic (Common)

The magic of the natural world. Fire, water, earth, air—these elements can be shaped by those with the gift. Elemental magic is relatively safe but limited. Most mages are elementalists.

**Users**: Common, regulated by the Circle

### Blood Magic (Forbidden)

The darkest art. Blood magic requires sacrifice—often literal blood, sometimes lives. It is powerful but corrupts the user. The Silent Church declares all blood magic heretical.

**Users**: Cult of the Hollow, desperate individuals

# PART II: CHARACTERS

## Character 1: Kira Nightwind

### Background

Kira was born into the **Nightwind Coven**—one of the ancient mage families that survived the Sundering. For two thousand years, the Nightwinds carried a secret: they were guardians of one piece of Aethon's crown.

When Kira was twelve, her parents were murdered by the Cult of the Hollow. They died protecting her, hiding her with a false identity in the remote village of **Thornwick**. She was told to hide her magic, live as a peasant, and never contact other mages.

That was eight years ago.

Now Kira lives in the shadows, practicing her void magic in secret, hunted by the Cult who know she exists. She has one goal: find the other pieces of the crown before the Cult does, and destroy them all.

But she's running out of time. The Cult found her in Thornwick. She barely escaped. Now she's heading to **Crownhaven**—the old imperial capital—where she believes one of the crown pieces is hidden in the catacombs beneath the palace.

### Personality

Kira is cold but not cruel. She has learned to trust no one, but she still feels the pull of connection. She is intelligent, strategic, and patient. She plans years ahead.

Underneath her cold exterior, she grieves. She misses her parents. She misses the person she could have been. She wonders if using void magic will eventually make her as hollow as the things her parents fought.

### Stats

- **Strength**: 4 (Physically weak from years of hiding)
- **Dexterity**: 6 (Quick reflexes from running)
- **Intelligence**: 8 (Sharp mind, analytical)
- **Charisma**: 5 (Intimidating, not charming)
- **Void Magic**: 7 (Natural talent, rusty from disuse)

### Skills

- **Shadow Walking**: Blend into shadows {REQUIRES: Void Magic 5}
- **Void Bolt**: Attack with pure un-energy {REQUIRES: Void Magic 3}
- **Mind Shield**: Protect against mental intrusion {REQUIRES: Void Magic 4}
- **Erase**: Remove something from existence {REQUIRES: Void Magic 8}

### Goals

1. Find all crown pieces
2. Destroy the Cult of the Hollow
3. Find out who truly killed her parents
4. Decide if she will use the crown or destroy it

## Character 2: Theron Ashford

### Background

Theron was the youngest knight ever appointed to the **Order of the Silver Shield**—the elite guard of the Valdorian royal family. At twenty-two, he was tasked with protecting **Princess Elara** during a diplomatic journey.

The journey was ambushed. Theron's squad was slaughtered. He was the only survivor—and he survived because he ran.

The princess was kidnapped. She was later rescued, but the damage was done. Theron was stripped of his rank, his lands were seized, and he was exiled from Valdoria. The knight who would have been the greatest of his generation became a beggar, a drunk, a nothing.

That was five years ago.

Now Theron wanders the Ashen Marches, working as a mercenary for the Watch. He has no honor left to lose. But when a job goes wrong and he discovers a Cult of the Hollow plot to assassinate the Queen of Valdoria, he faces a choice: run again, or finally stand and fight.

### Personality

Theron is haunted. He carries his failure like a physical weight. He drinks to forget, fights to feel something, and has stopped believing in his own redemption.

But underneath the self-destruction, the old Theron still exists—the man who cared about justice, about duty, about doing what was right. That man is buried deep, but not dead.

### Stats

- **Strength**: 8 (Expert swordsman, strong)
- **Dexterity**: 6 (Adept but aging)
- **Intelligence**: 5 (Street smart, book dumb)
- **Charisma**: 6 (Commanding presence when sober)
- **Combat**: 9 (Natural warrior)

### Skills

- **Blade Storm**: Multiple attacks {REQUIRES: Strength 7, Combat 7}
- **Inspire**: Rally allies {REQUIRES: Charisma 5}
- **Reckless Strike**: All-or-nothing attack {REQUIRES: Strength 8}

### Goals

1. Regain his honor (or define what honor means)
2. Protect the innocent
3. Confront his past (the princess, the Order)
4. Decide if he deserves redemption

## Character 3: Vex Shadowstep

### Background

Vex was born in the gutter of Theris. He learned to steal before he learned to read. At fourteen, he was recruited by the **Shadow Guild**—the greatest thieves' organization in the world.

For ten years, Vex was the Guild's best infiltrator. He stole secrets, not gold. He was trusted with the most dangerous jobs. He had a family—the Guild was his home.

Then he stole the wrong thing.

Vex was sent to penetrate the vaults of a mysterious collector in the Ashen Marches. What he found was a piece of Aethon's crown—and a trap. The Cult of the Hollow had been waiting. They wanted the piece, and they wanted Vex to deliver it to them.

Vex refused.

He escaped with the piece, but the Guild considered him a liability. They marked him for death. Now Vex is on the run, hunted by his former family, carrying a piece of the most dangerous artifact in the world.

His plan: find a safe buyer. Sell the piece to the highest bidder. Disappear.

But he's starting to wonder: is running really the answer?

### Personality

Vex is survival incarnate. He trusts no one, expects betrayal, and plans for every contingency. He is sarcastic, quick-witted, and uses humor as armor.

Underneath, he is grieving. The Guild was his family. Losing them—and knowing they want him dead—breaks him in ways he won't admit.

### Stats

- **Strength**: 5 (Agile, not strong)
- **Dexterity**: 9 (Master thief, incredible reflexes)
- **Intelligence**: 7 (Clever, resourceful)
- **Charisma**: 6 (Charming when necessary)
- **Stealth**: 10 (Unmatched)

### Skills

- **Lockpick**: Open any lock {REQUIRES: Dexterity 6}
- **Poison**: Create and apply toxins {REQUIRES: Intelligence 5}
- **Shadow Meld**: Become invisible in darkness {REQUIRES: Dexterity 8, Stealth 8}
- **Deadly Strike**: Instant kill from behind {REQUIRES: Dexterity 9, Stealth 7}

### Goals

1. Sell the crown piece safely
2. Find a new identity, a new life
3. Decide if revenge against the Guild is worth the risk
4. Choose between survival and purpose

## Character 4: Elara Vance

### Background

Elara was born into the **Vance Trading Company**—one of the wealthiest merchant families in Theris. At twenty, she was set to inherit the family empire. She had a mind for numbers, a talent for negotiation, and the ruthless ambition to make the Vance name immortal.

Then her parents died.

It looked like an accident—a carriage accident on a mountain road. Elara knew better. She saw the signs, the patterns, the cover-ups. Someone killed her parents for control of the Vance Company.

She couldn't prove it. She couldn't fight it. So she did the only thing she could: she ran. She abandoned the company, took a new name, and disappeared into the Ashen Marches.

Five years later, Elara has built a new network. She trades in information now—the same commodity the Shadow Guild deals in, but cleaner. She has contacts in every kingdom. She has money, influence, and a growing power base.

And she's close—closer than ever—to finding out who really killed her parents.

The trail leads to the Cult of the Hollow. And to a piece of Aethon's crown.

### Personality

Elara is calculating. She never acts without information. She never makes a promise she can't profit from. She is cold, controlled, and rarely shows emotion.

But grief drives her. The parents she loved are dead, and she will destroy whoever is responsible—even if it means destroying herself in the process.

### Stats

- **Strength**: 4 (Non-combatant)
- **Dexterity**: 5 (Adequate)
- **Intelligence**: 9 (Genius-level)
- **Charisma**: 8 (Master negotiator)
- **Influence**: 8 (Network of contacts)

### Skills

- **Information Network**: Gather intelligence {REQUIRES: Intelligence 6}
- **Negotiate**: Get what you want {REQUIRES: Charisma 6}
- **Forgery**: Create false documents {REQUIRES: Intelligence 5, Dexterity 4}
- **Business Empire**: Build economic power {REQUIRES: Intelligence 7, Charisma 6}

### Goals

1. Discover who killed her parents
2. Destroy them utterly
3. Rebuild the Vance Company
4. Decide if revenge is worth the cost
